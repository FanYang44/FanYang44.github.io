Data visualization with malaria data sets

Published: Fri 02 October 2020
By Fan Yang

In posts.

tags: data analysis

Data visualization tasks

Data visualization is a subset of data science. It helps people visualize data by using graphic representations. With effective visualizations, people are able to detect patterns, trends and relations in data. Thus, data visualization is also an essential step of data analysis.

In this post, three informative visualizations about malaria will be created using Python. Here are the three data sets that are going to be used in this post. They are from https://github.com/rfordatascience/tidytuesday/tree/master/data/2018/2018-11-13.

  • malaria_inc.csv - Malaria incidence by country for all ages across the world across time
  • malaria_deaths.csv - Malaria deaths by country for all ages across the world and time.
  • malaria_deaths_age.csv - Malaria deaths by age across the world and time.

The first figure shows us trends of deaths from malaria for different age groups all over the world. At a global level, children under 5 years old are the most vulnerable group to malaria deaths. The number of deaths from malaria tends to decrease with age.

The second figure focuses on top 10 countries with highest malaria incidences in 2000. The figure displays the trend of malaria incidences for these countries from 2000 to 2015 compared with world trend. In this group, although Turkey has the highest malaria incidence in 2000, it has the lowest incidence around 2008. All countries in this group have decreasing malaria incidences.
